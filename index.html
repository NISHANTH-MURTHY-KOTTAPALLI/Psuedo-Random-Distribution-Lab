Learn: What PRD is and why it exists

In games, “proc chance” effects can feel unfair with pure RNG: long dry streaks or unlikely back-to-back chains. Pseudo-Random Distribution (PRD) changes the *feel* by adjusting the chance based on prior failures.

True RNG (independent rolls)
Every attempt has the same probability p, regardless of what happened before. This is mathematically clean, but can produce streaks.
Proc-on-attempt-N: q(N) = (1-p)^(N-1) × p
PRD (adaptive chance)
The chance increases after each miss. When a proc happens, it resets. This reduces dry streaks and also reduces extreme lucky chains.
Dota-style: P(N) = min(1, C×N)
